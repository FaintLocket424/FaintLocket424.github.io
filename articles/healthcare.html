How Technology supports healthcare in developing countries

Technology has undoubtadly had a huge effect on healthcare. From complex medical devices like MRI scanners, to patient record databases, it is clear that computers have an enormous role in today's medical services.

There is, however, an emerging use of technology in healthcare, which you might not know is actually in use today!
Zipline is a logistics company who are aiming to bring drone delivery to the masses. While their marketing focusses heavily on food, grocery and other domestic deliveries, the real potential in this technology lies in improving logistics in healthcare. Zipline have been using their drones to deliver blood, vaccines and other time-critical medicine to remote hospitals and medical centres in Rwanda since 2016, and have recently expended into many other developing countries, plus some locations in Japan and the US.


Additionally, zipline claims their drones produce 97% less emissions per journey than a car, so would be a much faster and eco-friendly alternative to the current food delivery services such as Deliveroo and Uber Eats.

Youtuber Mark Rober covered this organisation in a video (embedded in this page) to raise awareness of the technology. Zipline proves that drone delivery is possible, practical and has the potential to transform the way we live our lives for the better.
